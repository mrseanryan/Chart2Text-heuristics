General Motors generated some 137 billion U.S. dollars in revenue during the 2019 fiscal year , a drop of 6.7 percent year-on-year . Revenue from GM 's automotive segments contracted by eight percent overall and 16 percent in markets outside North America . Investments , unions , and weak demand in Asia Automotive revenue suffered due to various factors , including a labor union strike in the U.S. , stalling vehicle sales , and high investments targeted at autonomous driving . Despite revenue of 100 million U.S. dollars , losses at GM 's Cruise subsidiary soared to more than one billion U.S. dollars in 2019 . As a result , GM 's EBIT margin dropped 1.9 points to 6.1 percent . Chevrolet crossovers performed well but were not able to offset falling vehicle sales in the Asia-Pacific region : Wuling and Baojun sales fell from almost two million to around 1.6 million units . The manufacturer 's overall vehicle sales declined by some eight percent , dropping to about 7.7 million vehicles in 2019 .
