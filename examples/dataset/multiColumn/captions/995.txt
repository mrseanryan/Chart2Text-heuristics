Essential oils have a voluminous import market in Europe . In 2017 , a total of 87,000 tonnes of essential oils were imported by European countries . On an individual level , countries within Europe had varying degrees of interest and import trade in different types of essential oils . Germany , for instance , was a major importer of orange oil , while Ireland was the importer of 31 percent of lemon oil . Heavy traffic of imports and exports of essential oils Essential oils are used for a variety of purposes , including the production of perfumes and fragrances , as well as being an elementary ingredient in aromatherapy . Globally , the leading countries that export essential oils are a geographical mix . Among them France stood out as the strongest export market in 2017 , exporting over 17 billion U.S. dollars worth essential oils and resinoids in that year . Europe : top region in the market With France at the helm as a major exporter , Europe held half of the global market for essential oils in 2017 . Indeed , since 2014 the total production value of essential oils in the EU rose dramatically , reaching over 1 bilion euros by 2017 .
