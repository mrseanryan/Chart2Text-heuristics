Mercedes-Benz cars were the most expensive automobiles sold in the EU in 2017 . With an average price tag of 47,243 euros , the car maker ranked ahead of fellow German manufacturer BMW . The only car brand which had seen its prices decrease since 2013 was Citroen . Mercedes-Benz is part of the Daimler AG and was the leading luxury car brand by global sales in 2018 . Car prices highest in Denmark Of countries within the EU , Denmark had the highest average car prices . In 2017 , Danish consumers had to pay 8,000 euros more than the EU-28 average , although consumers in Norway and Switzerland still paid more than in any EU countries . Mercedes-Benz European market share at 4.9 % Mercedes-Benz 's European market share in March 2019 amounted to 4.9 percent . This was 0.6 percent lower than in the same month the previous year . In the second half of 2018 , the leading passenger car brand in Europe was VW , with Mercedes-Benz ranking in fifth place .
