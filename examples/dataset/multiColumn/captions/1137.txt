The average age of passenger cars on the road in the European Union was 11.1 years . Between 2013 and 2017 , the age of the continent 's fleet steadily progressed , with the oldest motor vehicles being heavy commercial units . As of 2017 , the European Union housed 264.2 million registered passenger cars . This was the peak since the beginning of the reporting period in 1990 , when figures where nearly one hundred million units lower . Lithuanians drive oldest cars Of European countries , the oldest passenger cars were found in Eastern Europe , with the average car in Lithuania almost 17 years of age . Countries like Luxembourg and Austria , where disposable income was higher , had a comparatively younger fleet . World fleet comparison Across the Atlantic the car and light truck fleet had a slightly higher average age to that reported in the European Union . Light vehicles registered in the United States were on average 11.6 years old , compared to 10.9 years for the European fleet . Comparatively , passenger cars in Japan were noticeably newer .
