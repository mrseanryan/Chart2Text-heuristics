Those aged 16 to 24 are the most likely to have accessed the internet on a mobile in Great Britain during 2018 , according to the United Kingdom 's ( UK ) Office for National Statistics . Usage rates for those aged 25 to 34 increased slightly from 2018 to 2019 , reaching 97 percent . Smartphone ownership 95 percent of people aged 16 to 34 years in the UK owned a smartphone as of 2018 . While ownership rates decline with age , the share of people over the age of 55 who own a smartphone has increased . 42 percent of people in this age bracket owned a smartphone in 2016 , while this figure exceeded 50 percent for the first time in 2018 . Globally , it is expected that 40 percent of the population will own a smartphone by 2021 . Apple at the top of the tree Apple are the leading smartphone manufacture in the UK as of 2018 , holding a market share of 51.46 percent . Apple continue to generally increase their share of the market , increasing their stake from 46.01 percent in 2010 . Samsung held 28.86 percent of the market in 2018 .
